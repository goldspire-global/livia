# Loom #1 — "Get Liv answering your DMs in 5 minutes"

**Owner:** founder · **Length target:** 5:00 · **Status:** script v1, recording pending

This is the script the founder reads aloud while screen-sharing the dashboard. Every beat is timed. The recording itself is out of scope for Task #29 — that's a founder action once the script is approved.

**Pre-record checklist:**
- Browser cleared, only the dashboard tab open.
- Demo workspace fresh: `Settings → Demo & Data → Wipe my data`, then `Reload demo data`.
- Pick **Salon Aurora** in the business switcher.
- Phone in hand for the live SMS test.
- Quiet room. One take target — re-record if there's a cough or the dog barks.

---

## Beat-by-beat script

### 0:00 — 0:30 · Cold open + sign-in

> "Hey — {{founder_first_name}} here. In the next five minutes I'm going to set up Livia from scratch, paste in everything she needs to know about your business, send a real test message, and you'll watch Liv reply. If at any point this looks harder than a five-minute job, just stop the video — that means we screwed up and I want to know about it."

*(Click sign-in. Use Google. Land on the empty dashboard.)*

> "This is the dashboard the first time you open it. Empty cockpit, nothing scary. Top right — that's where you switch between businesses if you have more than one location."

### 0:30 — 1:30 · Paste your business knowledge

*(Click left rail → Settings → AI Assistant tab.)*

> "Everything Liv needs to be useful sits on this one screen. Four toggles, that's it."
>
> "**Enable Liv.** That's the master switch. Off means she doesn't reply, on means she does."
>
> "**Auto-book.** Off means Liv suggests slots and asks the customer to confirm. On means she goes ahead and books. Most shops start with this off for the first week."
>
> "**Tone.** Professional, friendly, or playful. Pick whichever sounds like you on a normal Tuesday."
>
> "**Knowledge.** This is the most important field on the page. This is where you tell Liv anything a new staff member would need to know on day one. Prices, house rules, what you don't do, deposits, parking — everything."

*(Type into the knowledge field, slowly enough to read on screen:)*

> ```
> We're closed Sundays and bank holidays.
> Standard haircut is €35, takes 45 minutes.
> We don't do bleaching after 5pm — book those before noon.
> Deposits are €10 for any service over €50.
> Parking is on Drury Street, two minutes' walk.
> ```

*(Click Save.)*

> "That's it. Liv now knows your business."

### 1:30 — 3:00 · Send a test message

*(Click left rail → public booking page link, copy URL, open in incognito tab.)*

> "Now I'm going to be a customer. This is the public booking page your customers see. Bottom right — that's the chat widget."

*(Click chat widget. Type:)*

> ```
> Hey, can I get a haircut tomorrow afternoon?
> ```

> "Notice the disclosure — Liv tells the customer up front that she's an automated assistant. That's not a marketing line, that's the EU AI Act, and it's wired in by default."

*(Wait for Liv's reply — should land in 3-5 seconds.)*

> "There. Liv has read your knowledge, checked your real availability, and offered slots. Watch what happens when I pick one."

*(Pick a slot, give a name + phone number, confirm.)*

> "Booking confirmed. The customer just got a confirmation message. Your calendar just gained an appointment. Total time from message to booking — about thirty seconds."

### 3:00 — 4:30 · Watch Liv reply (the owner's view)

*(Switch back to the dashboard tab → click left rail → Inbox.)*

> "This is the Inbox. Every conversation Liv has, you see in here. Live. The thread on the right is the one we just had — same conversation, your view of it."

*(Scroll the thread.)*

> "You can see everything she said, what she looked up, why she suggested those particular slots. Nothing is hidden."

*(Click "Take over" in the thread header.)*

> "Anytime you want, you take over. Liv stops replying, you're in the seat. The customer never knows there was a swap. When you're done, mark resolved and Liv waits for the next message."

*(Switch to the Cockpit page.)*

> "And the booking we just made — there it is on the timeline, right where it belongs. With a little badge that tells you Liv was the one who booked it."

### 4:30 — 5:00 · The honest close

*(Back to the Inbox view.)*

> "That's it. Five minutes from sign-in to first AI-handled booking. Three things to remember:
>
> One — start with auto-book *off* for your first week. Read what Liv would have done before she does it.
>
> Two — every Friday afternoon, take five minutes to update your knowledge field. New service, new staff, anything that changed.
>
> Three — if Liv ever says something you wouldn't say, screenshot it and send it to me. That's how we get better.
>
> If you've got questions, my number's in your welcome email. Talk soon."

*(End on the Inbox view. Don't fade out — just stop.)*

## Recording notes

- **One continuous take.** No splice cuts. Real product latency is part of the demo.
- **Speak slightly slower than feels natural.** Watching at 1x should feel like a calm conversation, not a tour.
- **Don't move the cursor while talking.** Move, then talk about what's now on screen.
- **Show the disclosure deliberately.** Pause for 1.5 seconds when Liv's first message lands so the viewer reads it.
- **End on confidence, not on a soft fade.** Cut on "talk soon."
