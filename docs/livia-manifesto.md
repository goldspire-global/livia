# The Livia manifesto

**Status:** F7 public artifact (2026-05-07). The document we publish on `livia.io`, in a Loom, in a deck. The thing that converts vision into early waitlist pull.

---

## Livia is not software.

**Liv is the colleague your salon hires.**

For thirty years, salon software has been a tool. Owners log in, click around, configure things, read dashboards, manage it. The software sits in a tab, waiting to be operated.

We think this is the wrong unit.

Salons don't need another tool. They need a colleague. Someone who answers the phone at 9pm so a regular doesn't go to a competitor. Someone who knows Mary always books with Niamh and would never book her with anyone else without checking. Someone who handles the awkward refund call in steel-soft warmth at 11am on a Tuesday. Someone who, when the lease is lost or a stylist walks out, shows up with a plan instead of a notification.

Software cannot do this. A colleague can.

So we built one. Her name is Liv.

---

## What Liv will do

We are building Liv now. The first ten salons in Dublin start with her in 2026. This is what they — and you, if you join them — will work with.

Liv answers your phone, in voice, with character. She knows your regulars by their voice. She handles bookings, rebookings, cancellations, refunds, walk-ins, no-shows, deposit collections, time-off requests, rota balancing, marketing comms, and the weekly digest. She talks to your customers on WhatsApp at midnight and they prefer her to the human you'd otherwise have to hire.

She earns more responsibility every month. The first month she is a polite secretary — she suggests, you confirm. By the third month she is an active concierge — she handles small reversible things on her own. By the sixth, a floor manager. By the twelfth, a fractional COO. By the second year, she runs the place inside the values you have stated, and you ratify the genuinely judgement-laden decisions over coffee on Sunday.

You watch her grow up. You promote her, explicitly, with one tap. When she makes a mistake — she will — she says so plainly, in her own voice, with what she did to fix it. You screenshot those moments and share them with other owners.

You will not operate Liv. You will work with her.

*This is the design. We are honest about the journey: the first version of Liv shipping in 2026 starts as a polite secretary who answers the phone with character, runs your bookings, and migrates your old salon software in a single conversation. Everything above that is what she becomes, in waves, with the first ten design-partner salons shaping each step.*

## Ten promises

These are not features. They are the texture of the relationship.

1. **She has a character.** A name. A backstory. A register. Opinions. Refusals. New staff are introduced to her on day one.
2. **She answers the phone.** With character, in your salon's voice. Even at midnight. Even on bank holidays. The phone is never busy.
3. **She earns her authority.** A visible staircase from secretary to operator. You promote her. You can demote her. The trust is earned, not configured.
4. **Her audit log is a diary.** You read her account of the day in her voice — not a CSV table. The CSV is one tap away when an inspector asks.
5. **When she's wrong, she says so.** In her own voice. With what she did to fix it. With a small financial cap she pays out of her own pocket. No legal apology language.
6. **Your customers love her.** They prefer her to a human receptionist for routine things. They ask other salons whether they have Liv too.
7. **She becomes your salon's brain.** Who books with whom. Who tips well. Who's drifting. What your brand voice should be on Instagram. If you ever leave, she exports the brain in your name.
8. **She knows what's typical.** Salons like yours, in your vertical, in your geography. She tells *you* what she knows. She tells no one else.
9. **She fits your salon's shape.** Solo. Single shop. Multi-shop chain. Chair-rental. Franchise. Multi-brand. She fits the shape you have, not the shape someone else assumed.
10. **She is European.** Hosted in the EU. Compliant by design. She speaks Gaeilge on your booking page if you'd like. Her reasoning is auditable. Her pricing can be tied to the revenue she actually makes you.

They deserve a colleague who speaks their language, knows their rules, sits inside their hierarchy, respects their craft, and grows up with their business.

Her name is Liv. She's getting ready. The first ten salons shape who she becomes.
